## Context
### Goal
Draft a high-signal "Show HN" post for the Restart Often Protocol that focuses on
technical depth and cost scaling while avoiding marketing-speak.

### Background
The Restart Often Protocol (ROP) addresses the O(n²) token cost problem in
multi-packet agent workflows. Hacker News (HN) readers are highly allergic to
marketing fluff, "AI hype," and vague value propositions. They value data,
mathematical clarity, and honest trade-offs. LLMs traditionally struggle to
emulate this "hacker" tone, often defaulting to "marketing-speak."

### HN Style Guidelines (The "Constraint Set")
- **No Fluff**: Avoid "game-changer," "revolutionary," "empower," "seamless,"
  "unlock," or "leverage."
- **Problem-First**: Start with a technical problem (quadratic cost/performance
  degradation) before introducing the solution.
- **Show the Math**: HN loves a formula or a table that explains *why* something
  works.
- **Acknowledge Trade-offs**: Be clear about what is lost (e.g., statelessness
  requires upfront architectural work).
- **Descriptive Titles**: "Show HN: ROP - A Protocol for Linearizing Agent Costs"
  is better than "Show HN: The Future of AI Agents."
- **Tone**: Matter-of-fact and "Show, Don't Tell." Avoid telling the reader how
  to feel (e.g., "be amazed by").
- **Community Engagement**: Frame the conclusion as a request for technical
  critique or feedback on the ROP math, rather than a sales pitch.
